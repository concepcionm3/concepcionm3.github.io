Gamification in Physical Education

Source 1

URL Evaluating the Effectiveness of Gamification in Physical Education: Enhancing Engagement Through Play and Movement
Author Çakir, T., Koldoshev, M. K., Asghar, S., Rajput, A., & Ahmed, S. M.
Abstract This study, published in the journal Retosl, defines the basics of what gamification is and how the researchers within this study plan on implementing those elements to observe how students ages 13 to 16 engage with their given tasks. By incorporating game-like mechanics such as rewards and a leaderboard into tasks, researchers were able to discover a significant increase in student engagement, which implies high levels of intrinsic motivation if following the criteria for Self-Determination Theory. From a physical standpoint, the average step count also went up. This source is just one of two articles that greatly explain how integrating gamification the right way can cultivate satisfactory results.

Source 2

URL Gamification in Physical Education: A Systematic Review
Author Arufe-Giráldez, V., Sanmiguel-Rodríguez, A., Ramos-Álvarez, O., & Navarro-Patón, R.
Abstract This article explores the use of gamification in physical education from kindergarten level to high school level and its impact on student performance, motivation, and engagement. The researchers integrated knowledge from various databases into their paper to solve this issue. Their findings show that gamification always boosts motivation, which increases student engagement during class. The usage of external rewards like badges played a heavy role in those results, which is expected when using gamification. This paper is an interesting read as it will provide insightful knowledge on how gamification functions in a class setting.
