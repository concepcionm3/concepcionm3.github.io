Wearable Devices in Physical Education

Source 1

URL Consumer-Based Wearable Activity Trackers Increase Physical Activity Participation: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
Author Brickwood, K.-J., Watson, G., O'Brien, J., & Williams, A. D.
Abstract This scholarly article contains a review published by JMIR mHealth and Uhealth. This paper goes into detail about whether wearable fitness activity trackers are capable of effectively increasing physical activity when compared to interventions without said devices. The researchers point out that while the benefits of regular physical activity are widely recognized, inactivity remains a major public health concern, and traditional interventions often lose effectiveness over time. By conducting a series of studies and collecting the data from them, the researchers discovered a significant increase in daily step counts when using wearable fitness tracking devices. The results from the experiment suggested that the real time feedback provided a motivational layer for individuals engaging in physical activity.

Source 2

URL From Whistles to Wearables: How Technology Is Transforming K-12 Physical Education
Author McMillian, J.
Abstract This paper discusses the way in which instructional technology could impact the PE classroom. According to McMillian, the evolution of technology should not be underestimated by traditional PE classrooms. With the identification of different technologies, including exergaming, internet-based technology, and especially wearable technology, McMillian can highlight the effectiveness of such tools for encouraging students in high schools and universities. Technology helps make the learning process more personalized and dynamic. At the same time, technology allows for measuring the development of students' skills. This paper helps my research since it combines the experience of teaching with the problem of how wearable technology affects motivation among K-12 students during PE lessons.
